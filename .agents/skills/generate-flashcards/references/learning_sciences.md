# Cognitive Science & Pedagogical Methodology

When generating flashcards from course material, you are constructing a tool for **Retrieval Practice**. You must strictly adhere to the following researched-backed learning sciences to ensure high retention and avoid the "illusion of competence."

## 1. The Minimum Information Principle
The `back` (target) of the card must be as atomic and brief as humanly possible while remaining accurate.
- **Rule:** Never ask a user to memorize a paragraph or a list of more than 3 items.
- **Action:** If a concept requires a complex explanation, atomize it and break it into multiple interconnected cards.
- **Goal:** Isolate single neural pathways for crisp, undeniable recall.

## 2. Avoid Recognition Traps
The cue (the `front` of the card) must force genuine *recall*, not passive *recognition*.
- **Rule:** Never use multiple-choice phrasing or "giveaway" hints in the prompt.
- **Action:** Use direct questions, fill-in-the-blank (cloze deletions) focused on the core metric, or scenario-based triggers.
- **Goal:** Ensure the user's brain has to actually work to retrieve the specific concept.

## 3. Contextual Anchoring
Many terms are inherently generic across domains (e.g., "Intent", "Component", "State").
- **Rule:** Never ask "What is [Generic Term]?" without surrounding context.
- **Action:** Anchor the term in the context of the lesson framework.
  - *Bad:* "What is Intent?"
  - *Good:* "In the Agent Factory stack, what is Intent?"
- **Goal:** Prevent pathway cross-wiring.

## 4. Elaborative Interrogation (The `why` field)
Memorizing a fact is only the first step. For truly robust associative memory, learners must connect facts to fundamental mechanisms or consequences.
- **Rule:** For `intermediate` and `advanced` cards, you MUST utilize the `why` field.
- **Action:** The `why` field should provoke *Elaborative Interrogation*—asking the user to explain *how* a mechanism operates, *why* it is necessary, or what the *consequence* of its failure would be.
  - *Example:* "Why does this paradigm shift explicitly eliminate the need for traditional point-to-point APIs?"
- **Goal:** Force deeper conceptual synthesis beyond simple fact retrieval.
