### Core Concept

The four deal-focused and research-focused Agent Skills — Due Diligence Data Pack, Company Teaser, Earnings Analysis, and Initiating Coverage Report — follow the same pattern as the market-analysis skills from Lesson 4: they connect to live data sources, produce structured professional deliverables with cell-level transparency, and compress hours of manual work into minutes. The critical difference is that these skills operate in contexts where **judgment matters more than calculation** — the analyst's job shifts from producing numbers to deciding what the numbers mean.

### Key Mental Models

- **Due Diligence as Question Surfacing**: Ratios are the starting point, not the deliverable. The data pack surfaces anomalies (DSO expansion, low cash flow conversion, customer concentration); the professional decides which anomalies require investigation, which are explainable, and which should affect the transaction price.
- **Teasers as Marketing**: A teaser is a marketing document, not a financial model. Claude handles structure and drafting; the banker's judgment is in how the business is positioned — the framing that drives NDA signings, not the financial metrics themselves.
- **Earnings as Expectations**: Stock prices move on results relative to what was already expected, not on absolute performance. The beat/miss pattern — which lines beat and which missed — reveals where the business is performing ahead of or behind the market's embedded assumptions.
- **Initiating Coverage as Insight**: The framework and models are standard. What differentiates an initiation is the non-consensus insight — something the analyst believes about the company that the market is not pricing. Claude builds the model; the analyst supplies the view.

### Critical Patterns

- Every skill produces outputs with traceable cell references — when a client asks why an estimate changed, the analyst can show exactly which cell changed and why
- The skills compress the data-processing half of professional workflows (60-70% of elapsed time), leaving the analyst more time for the judgment half
- Claude does not provide investment advice, does not validate assumptions, does not replace an audit, and makes mistakes that must be verified — these are structural boundaries, not version limitations

### Common Mistakes

- Treating the Due Diligence data pack as the diligence itself — the pack surfaces questions, but deciding which questions matter is the professional's work
- Submitting AI-generated earnings notes without adding qualitative perspective — the data processing is commodity; the judgment framing is the value
- Confusing the Initiating Coverage framework with a finished initiation — the skill produces the structure and populated data (60% of the work); the thesis, risk assessment, and non-consensus view (40%) remain the analyst's contribution
